The Global Gender Pay Gap

According to the World Economic Forum, the global pay gap between men and women will take 202 years to close at the slow pace it's moving at currently. The WEF found that on average, women across the world are paid just 63% of what men earn. The most shocking fact is that there is not a single country where women are paid as much as men.

The country that comes the closest to closing the gap is Laos, in south east Asia, where women are earning 91% of what men are paid. The largest pay gaps exist in Yemen, Syria, and Iraq where women are paid less than 30% of men's wages. The world clearly has a long way to go in closing the gender pay gap and truly transforming society for the better.
